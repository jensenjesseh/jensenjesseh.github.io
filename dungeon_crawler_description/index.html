Dungeon Crawler Game

Team project with Kylie Williamson and Aaron Lam

This game was made over the course of three-to-four weeks. It was coded from scratch (with the exception of the Box2DJS physics engine) using JavaScript and HTML 5.

This project has the following characteristics:

  • Procedurally generated
  • Reconfigurable controls
  • Particle Effects
  • Frustum culling (using a quad-tree data structure)
  • Tile Rendering
  • 2D Camera
  • 2D Physics integration (uses Box2DJS physics engine)
  • Server-based high-scores
  • Enemy AI
  • Player progression (level-ups)
  • Save/load game functionality
  • Enemy triggering

Notes about this project:

While my team and I collectively worked on most of the features of the game, I was directly over the frustum culling and quad-tree functionality that prevents enemies outside of the player's view to update and render. This makes the program more efficient. I was also directly over the enemy AI, enemy triggering, and sound and music. My team and I made all of the original artwork. The music was not made by the team but is from a creative commons source.
